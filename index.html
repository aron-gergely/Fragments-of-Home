FRAGMENTS OF HOME

ABOUT

This project serves as an exploration of the hybrid essay, a form that blends various media to present fragmented narratives. By combining text and images in a non-linear, random manner, the project mimics the hybrid essay's open-ended and associative nature. Like a collage, the project encourages users to experiment with narrative construction, revealing how seemingly unrelated elements can coexist and interact to create new meanings, interpretations, and visual stories.

The content of the project centers around the themes of home, organizing, and everyday domestic life. Texts and images are sourced from online communities, such as Reddit threads and Facebook groups, where people share personal advice, frustrations, and reflections on managing their spaces. These materials, once decontextualized and paired at random, take on new unexpected associations. The randomness mirrors the often chaotic, improvised nature of organizing a home, while the act of arranging these pairs on a virtual canvas invites users to create their own systems of meaning. Whether through thematic groupings, visual rhythms, or narrative fragments, this second layer of interaction echoes how we make sense of messiness — both in our environments and in the stories we tell.

GUIDE

01Create image-text pairs using the [New Image] and [New Text] buttons, or generate random pairs by clicking [Randomize].

02If you feel even more inspired, you can also rewrite the text and upload your own images.

03Add these combinations to your workspace (on the right) with the arrow button [➔].

04Organize them to your liking: Drag them around, create new associations or stories by experimenting with the layout.

05Additionally, you can [Save] your layout as a PDF.
